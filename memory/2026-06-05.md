## FIA Doc 32 - Decision - Car 30 (Liam Lawson) - Alleged leaving the pitlane after the end of the session
**Date:** 05 June 2026, 16:41 UTC
**Title:** Decision - Car 30 - Alleged leaving the pitlane after the end of the session
**Sender:** The Stewards (Garry Connelly, Derek Warwick, Tanja Geilhausen, Jean-François Calmes)
**To:** Visa Cash App Racing Bulls F1 Team Manager

### Incident Details
- **Driver:** Liam Lawson (Car #30, Visa Cash App Racing Bulls F1 Team)
- **Incident Time:** 14:30 during Free Practice 1
- **Fact:** Car 30 left the pit lane when the light at the end of the pit lane was red
- **Infringement:** Breach of Article B1.6.3e of FIA F1 Regulations

### Hearing Process
- Stewards heard from driver, team representatives
- Reviewed positioning/marshalling system data, video, timing, team radio and in-car video evidence

### Decision
- **No penalty is applied**

### Reasoning
**Timeline:**
1. Lawson warned he had 7 seconds left when he left the garage
2. Travelled down the pit lane
3. Green light changed to red when he was approximately 0.8 seconds from crossing the pit lane exit
4. Lawson reported focusing on the countdown clock above the pit exit, which showed 2 seconds remaining before the light turned red

**Key Points:**
- Countdown clock is **NOT official** and does not override the red light at pit exit
- No official synchronization between countdown clock and red light
- Driver had less than a second to react
- If he tried to stop, he might have ended up coming to a halt on a live track (more dangerous)

### Mitigating Circumstances
- Misleading countdown clock information (showed 2 seconds but light was already red)
- Extremely short reaction time (<1 second)
- Safety consideration: stopping on live track would be more dangerous

### Key Observations
- Lawson's third incident of the day (Doc 30 impeding, Doc 31 double yellows, Doc 32 pit lane red light)
- No penalties for any of the three incidents
- Highlights potential issue with pit lane countdown clock synchronization

**Sender:** Manuel Leal (FIA Formula 1 Technical Delegate)

### Details
- **Car:** Car 01 (Lando Norris, McLaren Mastercard F1 Team)
- **Incident:** Car 01 stopped on the track during FP2
- **Problem:** Marshals pressed the CDS button, but CDS was NOT working as required
- **Breach:** Formula 1 Regulations, Article C9.3
- **Action:** Technical Delegate refers matter to Stewards for consideration

### What is CDS?
- CDS = Car Data System
- Mandatory real-time data transmission system on all F1 cars
- Functions:
  - Transmits vehicle position, speed, status to FIA
  - Auto-triggers warnings in emergencies (like stopping on track)
  - Assists marshals and Race Control for quick response
- **If CDS fails when car stops on track, marshals cannot confirm vehicle status, potentially delaying rescue or creating safety hazards**
